Explanation of the data
First, we looked at groups that had at least 2 mentions of Josh Lincoln between 1.8.2025 and 1.10.2025. We exported all the messages from these groups → masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv.


We then added more data about all user profiles appearing in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv (as authors of messages or reactors to messages).
For each user, we exported:
1. All the groups they are a member of → masked_user_groups_data.csv
2. All the reactions they made across all the groups they are members of (reminder - masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv only contains data from groups with at least 2 Lincoln mentions. Many of the users are active also in groups that didn’t have 2 or more Lincoln mentions) → masked_user_reactions.csv
3. All the messages they sent across all the groups they are members of → masked_user_messages.csv


Masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv
All messages from all the groups that had at least two messages mentioning Lincoln between 1.8.2025 and 1.10.2025. 
The columns:
* maskedGroupId
* description - description of the group
* subject - subject of the group
* memberCount - number of members in the group
* creationTimestamp - when the group was created
* announcementOnly - whether the group is readonly or discussion
* joinedAt - when we joined the group
* political_leaning - our tag of the political leaning of the group (if applicable)
* demographic - our tag of the targeted demographic of the group (if applicable)
* topic - our tag of the topic of the group
* organization - our tag of the organization the group belongs to (if applicable)
* organization_type - our tag of the type of the organization the group belongs to (if applicable)
* age - our tag of the targeted age group of the group (if applicable)
* region - our tag of the geographical region the group targets (if applicable)
* maskedMessageId - the ID of the message (masked)
* maskedAuthorId - the ID of the author of the message (masked)
* timestamp - when the message was sent
* body - the body of the message
* maskedMessageReply - the IDs of the reply messages to the original message (if exists) (masked)
* maskedReactions - the reactions to the message (if exists) (masked)
* maskedQuotedMessageId - the ID of the message that this message is a reply to (if exists) (masked)
* forwardingScore - the number of times the message was forwarded
* mediaHash - the hash of the attached media (if exists)
* mediaMimeType - the mimetype of the attached media (if exists)
* num_Lincoln_messages - the number of messages mentioning  Lincoln in the group in the specified time period
* percent_Lincoln_messages - the percentage of messages in the group mentioning Lincoln in the specified time period
Masked_user_groups_data.csv
User group memberships, for all the users appearing in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv (i.e. for each user appearing in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv, a list of all the groups they are a member of).
Columns that hold attributes of the group:
* description
* subject
* memberCount
* creationTimestamp
* announcementOnly
* membershipApproval
* joinedAt - when rakemet joined the group
* memberAddMode
* tags.politicalLeaning.tagValue
* tags.demographic.tagValue
* tags.topic.tagValue
* tags.organization.tagValue
* tags.organization.organizationType
* tags.demographic.age
* tags.demographic.gender
* tags.organization.organizationName
* masked_groupId
* tags.geographic


Columns that hold attributes of the user:
* firstName
* masked_userId
* groups.role
* groups.status


Masked_user_reactions.csv
All reactions made by all the users appear in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv (across all our data - including in groups that aren’t in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv).


Masked_user_messages.csv
All messages written by all the users appear in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv (across all our data - including in groups that aren’t in masked_Lincoln_messages_output.csv).
